Project: Scratch
Course: Computer Science Principles
Duration: August 30th - September 9th 2018
Objective: Create a fun and entertaining game
Type: Pair project

The Scratch Project was my First project in this course. We used the programming interface Scratch. Our game was almost like a running race spanning across multiple screens. To make the game a challenge we added boxes across the screen so the runner also had to avoid them. As an incentive and a form of a score for the player they had to collect 3 stars. Before you finished the level the player was taken to a screen that said: “you may only pass if you have all 3 stars” making collecting the starts are a requirement. I played an essential role in this project because of my motivation. Towards the end of the project, my partner and I were having trouble setting the score feature for our game. I was so motivated outside of the class time I tried to fix it at home.After a productive 30 minutes I learned I was sticking the score variable in the wrong loop making the score count infinitely. In this project, I learned the virtue of patience and delegation. We alternated different portions of code. While I was working on the score, my partner worked on the boxes, stars, and backgrounds. Also, we had to have the patience to work with scratch because it does not tell you your errors so we had to look at it ourselves. The task was unpleasant but we figured it out.
