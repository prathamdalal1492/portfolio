Scratch

Course: Computer Science Principles
Duration: August 30th - September 9th 2018
Objective: Create a fun and entertaining game
Type: Pair project

The Scratch Project was my First project in this course. We used the programming interface Scratch. Our game was almost like a running race spanning across multiple screens. To make the game a challenge we added boxes across the screen so the runner also had to avoid them. As an incentive and a form of a score for the player they had to collect 3 stars. Before you finished the level the player was taken to a screen that said: “you may only pass if you have all 3 stars” making collecting the starts are a requirement. I played an essential role in this project because of my motivation. Towards the end of the project, my partner and I were having trouble setting the score feature for our game. I was so motivated outside of the class time I tried to fix it at home.After a productive 30 minutes I learned I was sticking the score variable in the wrong loop making the score count infinitely. In this project, I learned the virtue of patience and delegation. We alternated different portions of code. While I was working on the score, my partner worked on the boxes, stars, and backgrounds. Also, we had to have the patience to work with scratch because it does not tell you your errors so we had to look at it ourselves. The task of finding the errors in the code was unpleasant but with teamwork we figured it out. On the left side I have embeded the full game. For my Documentation click here

Type: Pair project

This project is the photo manipulation project. Our objective was to create a script to assist people edit their photos in a professional manner. Our theme was Juan’s submission for the Film Festival, called Midterms. In an effort to promote we created photos that would have filters and masks promoting this movie. This project taught me how to work through errors and not give up. Me and my partner went through many technical and emotional struggles. A couple days my partner was absent which made it difficult to do the project and communicate. These days happened to be the worst in terms of errors. Cloud 9, the environment we worked in had problems running our images. When we tried to output a PNG there would be no changes to the image. It was at this time I learned the powerful effect of asking for help. I asked Apollo for help, and together we solved this problem. The next day Juan came back and we ran into more obstacles. When we tried to add a mask, the image did show these changes. We spent 40 minutes trying to figure it out. At the end of consulting with classmates we realized our program was killing it’s self because the images were too complex. I think me and juan both exemplified leadership qualities. Both of us were absent at different times during the project. In order to do well both of us communicated with each other after class. If we were not able to make progress in class we would work on it at home. Additionally we would stay up to date on our documentation, which was our most valued deliverable.
